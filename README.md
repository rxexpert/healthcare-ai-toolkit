# healthcare-ai-toolkit

**healthcare-ai-toolkit** is a curated collection of model‑agnostic AI skills, guardrails, and guidance designed to support the safe, effective, and responsible use of generative AI in healthcare workflows.

This repository focuses on **guided use of AI**, emphasizing safety, clarity, and human oversight rather than automation or autonomous decision‑making.

---

## Purpose

Generative AI can significantly improve efficiency and quality in healthcare—but only when used thoughtfully and safely.

This toolkit exists to:
- Reduce risk associated with AI‑generated content in healthcare contexts
- Encode domain‑informed guardrails into reusable AI skills
- Enable consistent, high‑quality outputs across different AI platforms
- Support real‑world clinical, administrative, and governance workflows

---

## What This Repository Provides

This repository is meant to provide collection of **structured AI usage artifacts** intended to guide and constrain the application of generative AI in healthcare settings. Majority of the content is focused for medication management / Pharmacy. 

These artifacts may include (but are not limited to):
- **Agentic instructions** and behavioral guardrails for AI systems
- **Reusable skill‑like patterns** for common healthcare workflows
- **Domain‑informed guidance** for handling clinical and medication‑related content
- **Safety‑aware content conventions** for documentation, communication, and review tasks
- **Examples and templates** that demonstrate responsible, human‑in‑the‑loop AI usage

While attempt is made to write content to be **as platform‑agnostic as reasonably possible** and intended to be adaptable across multiple generative AI tools (Microsoft 365 Copilot, Claude, Gemini, etc), due to differences in models, system prompts, and platform constraints, out‑of‑the‑box compatibility is not guaranteed. Adaptation and validation are expected.


---

## What This Repository Does NOT Provide

- Clinical decision‑making or treatment recommendations  
- Patient‑specific medical advice  
- Replacement for professional judgment, institutional policy, or regulatory requirements  

All tools in this repository **require human review** and are intended to augment—not replace—trained healthcare professionals.

---

## Initial Skill Areas

### Medication Safety & Clinical Communication
Skills focused on reducing ambiguity and risk in medication‑related content, such as:
- Safe medication nomenclature
- Avoidance of error‑prone abbreviations
- Clear representation of doses, units, and routes
- Preservation of source context without inference

### Governance, Documentation, and Operations
Skills designed to support healthcare committees and administrative processes, including:
- Committee meeting minute generation
- Policy drafting and review assistance
- Structured capture of decisions, votes, and action items
- Alignment with professional healthcare guidance (e.g., ASHP‑informed practices)
